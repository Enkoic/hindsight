# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

This project is small, technical, and privacy-sensitive. The goal of this code of conduct is simple: keep collaboration direct, respectful, and useful.

## Expected behavior

- criticize ideas, code, and assumptions without attacking people
- use clear, concrete technical arguments
- respect privacy; do not pressure others to share raw transcripts, secrets, or personal activity data
- accept correction when you are wrong and update your position accordingly
- keep discussions focused on improving the project for users and contributors

## Unacceptable behavior

- harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- discriminatory or demeaning language
- repeated bad-faith argumentation, trolling, or deliberate derailment
- publishing someone else's private logs, prompts, credentials, or personal data without consent
- using project spaces to market unrelated products or services

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that violate these expectations. They may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation when needed to protect contributors and the project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness a serious conduct problem, contact the maintainer privately through GitHub. Include links, screenshots, or other concrete evidence where possible. Reports will be handled as confidentially as practical.

## Enforcement approach

The default approach is to de-escalate quickly and explain the boundary clearly. Repeated or severe violations may lead to comment removal, issue/PR closure, temporary muting, or a permanent ban from project spaces.
